30 Useless Facts That Will Actually Blow Your Mind

Published: March 22, 2026 ~10 min read By: UselesslyInteresting

Look, nobody woke up today thinking "I desperately need to know about lobster anatomy." And yet here you are. Welcome. Below you'll find 30 facts so wonderfully pointless, so beautifully irrelevant, that they might just be the most interesting things you read all week. No homework assignments. No quizzes. Just pure, uncut trivia straight to the brain.

Fact 01 / 30

Lobsters Pee Out of Their Faces

Lobsters have urine-release nozzles located right under their eyes. They squirt pee at each other as a form of communication, particularly during courtship. So the next time someone says romance is dead, point them to the lobster tank.

Nothing says "I love you" quite like a targeted stream of face-pee.

Fact 02 / 30

Scotland's National Animal Is the Unicorn

Since the 12th century, Scotland has proudly claimed the unicorn as its national animal. It was chosen as a symbol of purity and power in Celtic mythology. The Scots apparently decided that real animals were too boring.

When your country is this cool, you don't need a real animal.
History
Fact 03 / 30

Bananas Are Berries, but Strawberries Aren't

Botanically speaking, a banana qualifies as a berry because it develops from a single ovary and contains seeds within the flesh. Strawberries, on the other hand, are "accessory fruits" because their seeds are on the outside. Botany has zero respect for common sense.

Every smoothie you've ever made was a lie.
Nature
Fact 04 / 30

Astronauts Get Taller in Space

Without gravity compressing the spine, astronauts can grow up to 2 inches taller during a space mission. The downside? They shrink right back down upon returning to Earth. Space gives you height, then immediately takes it back like a cosmic Indian giver.

The universe's most elaborate growth spurt, with a 100% refund policy.
Space
Fact 05 / 30

Honey Never Spoils -- Ever

Archaeologists have found 3,000-year-old honey in Egyptian tombs that was still perfectly edible. Honey's low moisture content, acidic pH, and natural hydrogen peroxide make it basically immortal. It will outlast you, your grandkids, and probably civilization.

The only food with a longer shelf life than your unread emails.
Science
Fact 06 / 30

There's a Town Called "Boring" in Oregon

Boring, Oregon is a real place, and in 2012 it became a sister city to Dull, Scotland. Then Bland Shire in Australia joined the trio. Together they formed an alliance that is genuinely the opposite of what their names suggest.

The Boring-Dull-Bland trifecta is, ironically, the most interesting municipal partnership on Earth.

Fact 07 / 30

Cows Have Best Friends and Get Stressed When Separated

Research from Northampton University found that cows form intense bonds with specific herd members and show measurable stress responses -- elevated heart rates and cortisol levels -- when separated from their bestie. They also produce more milk when they're near their preferred companion. Cows invented the buddy system before summer camp did.

Your steak had a social life more meaningful than most group chats.
Animals
Fact 08 / 30

The Moon Has Better Internet Than Most of Rural Earth

In 2013, NASA beamed data to the Moon at 622 megabits per second using a pulsed laser system. That's faster than what millions of people get at home. The Moon doesn't even have residents, and it has better Wi-Fi infrastructure potential than half of Nebraska.

Somewhere, an astronaut is streaming in 4K while you buffer on YouTube.
Space
Fact 09 / 30

Wombat Poop Is Cube-Shaped

Wombats produce distinctly cubic feces, and scientists spent years figuring out why. Turns out, their intestines have varying elasticity that shapes the droppings into cubes. They stack them to mark territory, because round poop would just roll away.

Nature spent millions of years of evolution to engineer a cube-shaped turd. Respect.
Animals
Fact 10 / 30

The Last Execution by Guillotine in France Was the Same Year Star Wars Came Out

Hamida Djandoubi was executed by guillotine on September 10, 1977 -- the same year "Star Wars: A New Hope" hit theaters. While audiences were watching lightsabers, France was still using an 18th-century blade. The guillotine wasn't officially retired until 1981 when France abolished the death penalty entirely.

Luke Skywalker and the guillotine coexisted in the same timeline. History is unhinged.
History
Fact 11 / 30

A Cloud Can Weigh Over a Million Pounds

The average cumulus cloud contains around 1.1 million pounds of water. It floats because that weight is spread across a massive volume and the droplets are tiny enough to be suspended by rising air. Clouds are basically invisible sumo wrestlers.

That fluffy thing that looks like a bunny? It weighs more than 100 elephants.
Nature
Fact 12 / 30

Nintendo Was Founded When Jack the Ripper Was Still Active

Nintendo was established on September 23, 1889, as a playing card company in Kyoto, Japan. Jack the Ripper's murders had occurred just the year before in London. The company that would eventually create Mario started out making hand-painted hanafuda cards in the Victorian era. It took them about a century to discover plumbers could be profitable.

From Victorian card games to Mario Kart -- that's the longest corporate glow-up in history.
History
Fact 13 / 30

There Are More Possible Chess Games Than Atoms in the Observable Universe

The Shannon number estimates roughly 10^120 possible chess games. The observable universe contains about 10^80 atoms. Chess is more complex than physical reality, which probably explains why your uncle takes 45 minutes per move.

A 64-square board contains more possibilities than all the matter that exists. No pressure.
Science
Fact 14 / 30

Dolphins Have Names for Each Other

Each bottlenose dolphin develops a unique signature whistle that functions as a name. Other dolphins use that specific whistle to call out to them. They even remember the "names" of dolphins they haven't seen in 20 years. Dolphin reunions must be emotional.

They remember names better than you remember your coworker from two desks over.
Animals
Fact 15 / 30

Venus Spins Backward Compared to Most Planets

While most planets rotate counterclockwise when viewed from above, Venus rotates clockwise. Scientists think a massive ancient collision may have flipped it over. So on Venus, the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east -- the ultimate contrarian move.

Venus woke up one morning and chose chaos. Absolute icon behavior.
Space
Fact 16 / 30

Your Body Produces Enough Saliva to Fill Two Swimming Pools in a Lifetime

The average human produces between 0.5 and 1.5 liters of saliva per day. Over a typical lifetime, that adds up to roughly 25,000 liters -- enough to fill two standard swimming pools. Your mouth is running a 24/7 waterworks operation, and you never once got a utility bill for it.

You are, quite literally, a saliva fountain masquerading as a functioning adult.
Science
Fact 17 / 30

A Group of Pugs Is Called a "Grumble"

The official collective noun for a group of pugs is "a grumble." Given their perpetually disgruntled facial expressions and the wheezy breathing sounds they make, this might be the most accurately named collective noun in the English language. Whoever coined it deserves a linguistics award.

Three or more pugs is technically a complaint department. The language checks out.
Animals
Fact 18 / 30

There's a Lake in Australia That's Bright Pink

Lake Hillier on Middle Island, Western Australia, is a vivid bubblegum pink, and nobody fully understands why. The leading theory involves a combination of algae and halophilic bacteria, but the lake keeps its exact recipe a secret. It stays pink even if you take the water out.

Earth decided to make a lake that looks like a melted popsicle, and science just shrugged.
Nature
Fact 19 / 30

The Inventor of the Pringles Can Is Buried in One

Fredric Baur designed the iconic Pringles tube and was so proud of his creation that he requested his ashes be buried in one. His family honored the wish in 2008. They reportedly debated which flavor for a while before settling on Original.

Once you pop, you literally don't stop -- not even death stops this man's brand loyalty.

Fact 20 / 30

Sunlight Reaching Earth Right Now Is 170,000 Years Old

A photon generated in the Sun's core takes an average of 170,000 years to bounce its way to the surface due to constant absorption and re-emission. Once it hits the surface, it takes only 8 minutes to reach Earth. The cosmic commute is mostly traffic.

That sunshine on your face left the Sun's core when Neanderthals were still around. No big deal.
Space
Fact 21 / 30

If You Shuffle a Deck of Cards Properly, the Order Has Almost Certainly Never Existed Before

A standard 52-card deck has 52! (52 factorial) possible arrangements -- that's roughly 8 x 10^67 unique orders. That number is so absurdly large that every properly shuffled deck in human history has almost certainly produced an arrangement that never existed before and will never exist again. Your last game of poker was a cosmic premiere.

Every card shuffle is a universe-first event. You're basically a chaos artist every game night.
Science
Fact 22 / 30

The Eiffel Tower Grows About 6 Inches Every Summer

Thermal expansion causes the iron in the Eiffel Tower to expand when heated by the Sun, making it grow roughly 15 centimeters in summer. It also leans slightly toward the shade side. The most famous tower in the world gets taller in warm weather and slouches like a teenager.

Even the Eiffel Tower has a summer glow-up.
Science
Fact 23 / 30

Pigeons Can Do Math

Researchers at the University of Otago found that pigeons can learn abstract numerical rules and rank groups of objects by quantity. They performed roughly on par with primates in numerical tasks. The bird you shoo away from your sandwich is doing arithmetic in its head.

Somewhere, a pigeon is judging your tip calculation at a restaurant.
Animals
Fact 24 / 30

There Was a War Between Australia and Emus -- and the Emus Won

In 1932, the Australian military was deployed with Lewis guns to cull about 20,000 emus destroying crops in Western Australia. After weeks of engagement, they had killed only a fraction of the birds. The operation was deemed a failure. The emus went undefeated.

A standing army with machine guns lost to birds who can't even fly. Peak Australia.
History
Fact 25 / 30

Trees Can Communicate Through Underground Fungal Networks

Dubbed the "Wood Wide Web," trees use mycorrhizal fungal networks to share nutrients, send chemical warning signals about pests, and even nurture their seedlings. A dying tree will sometimes dump its resources into the network for its neighbors. Trees are basically running a socialist underground economy.

Forests had the internet before we did. They just didn't use it for cat videos.
Nature
Fact 26 / 30

Your Nose Can Remember 50,000 Different Scents

The human olfactory system can distinguish at least 50,000 different smells, and some researchers argue the real number is closer to a trillion. Your nose is quietly running one of the most sophisticated chemical analysis labs on the planet, and you mostly use it to decide if milk has gone bad.

A trillion-scent supercomputer, reduced to the milk sniff test. Humanity at its finest.
Science
Fact 27 / 30

There's a Species of Jellyfish That's Essentially Immortal

Turritopsis dohrnii, the "immortal jellyfish," can revert its cells back to their juvenile state when stressed or old. It essentially restarts its life cycle and can theoretically do this forever. It's the only known animal that has figured out biological respawning.

This jellyfish looked at aging and said "nah, I'm good." We're still waiting for the tutorial.
Animals
Fact 28 / 30

The Shortest Complete Sentence in English Is "Go."

"Go" is a grammatically complete sentence with an implied subject ("you") and a verb. It contains a subject, predicate, and expresses a complete thought. Two letters, infinite authority. Shakespeare could never be this efficient.

Entire novels have failed to convey what one two-letter word accomplishes. Brevity wins.

Fact 29 / 30

Space Has a Distinct Smell

Astronauts consistently report that space smells like a combination of seared steak, gunpowder, and raspberries. The scent is caused by dying stars releasing polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons. The void of existence smells like a barbecue -- and honestly, that's kind of comforting.

The cosmos is out here smelling like a cookout. The universe knows what's up.
Space
Fact 30 / 30

The Total Length of All the Blood Vessels in Your Body Would Circle Earth Twice

If you laid out all the arteries, veins, and capillaries in an adult human body end to end, they'd stretch about 60,000 miles -- enough to wrap around the planet 2.5 times. Most of that length comes from the capillaries, which are so thin that blood cells travel through them single file.

You're basically a planet-wrapping blood highway disguised as a person. Walk accordingly.
